Our Popular Requests

Popular dishes and drinks that our NUS community loves

01.

Gongcha's
" Pearl Milk Tea"

Pearl milk tea (珍珠奶茶), sometimes referred to as Bubble Tea or Boba, is a Taiwanese drink combines milk tea with tapioca-type balls. The balls are also known as pearls, so the Chinese translation of 珍珠奶茶 is literally pearl (珍珠) milk (奶) tea (茶). The sweet taste of the tea paired with the chewy tapioca balls make up the unique element that boba provides. There really is no other drinks like it.

$3.50 "Help Me Dabao, Pls!"

02.

Sin Kee's
" Chicken Rice"

Sin Kee Chicken Rice is a relatively new addition to the plethora of food at The Deck in the Arts faculty. It is easy to identify the stall with its long queue during peak hours that very often extends and significantly blocks the main passage of the centre of the lower deck. This humble chicken rice stall used to be located at Margaret Drive Food Centre and has become a sensation among NUS students, with some of my friends literally eating it on a daily basis. Priced at $2.30 per plate, you can add anything you want and it will probably remain below $5.

$3 "Help Me Dabao, Pls!"

03.

Waa Cow!'s
" Signature Wagyu Beef Bowl"

If you’re ever planning on indulging on something as rich and luxurious as a Wagyu bowl, we urge you not to waste your calories (and money), and order Waa Cow! for one heck of a memorable meal.

$16.9 "Help Me Dabao, Pls!"

04.

FASS The Deck's
" Yong Tau Foo"

Having been featured in various media, the stall still maintain their pricing and quality. The stall offers a scrumptious meal with extremely affordable price. One bowl of yong tau foo normally cost around $3-4. Sounds too good to be true? Just order!

$3.5 "Help Me Dabao, Pls!"

05.

Starbucks's
" Java Chip Frappuccino"

This frappuccino is best-suited for people who like the taste of coffee (and not people who order a frappuccino so that they don’t have to taste the coffee)! As Starbucks itself explains, baristas make this drink by blending mocha sauce and Frappuccino chips with coffee, milk, and ice. Then, of course, they top the drink with whipped cream and mocha drizzle.

$8 "Help Me Dabao, Pls!"
